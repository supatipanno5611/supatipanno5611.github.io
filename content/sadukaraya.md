### Worship of the Lord Buddha, that relieves worries and cultivates virtues

Have been prepared in association with the Buddhist sermons which revived the previously hidden Noble Dhamma doctrine.

The ‘Buddha śāsana’ (the dispensation of the Lord Buddhā) shines/thrives when the Noble Dhamma doctrine is open and not when covered⋯

### Dedication
May this booklet be a tribute to the late Venerable *Waharaka Abhayaratanālankāra Thero* for fulfilling his quest for the revival of the Noble Path towards Nirvāna, during a time-period within the ‘Gōtama Buddha śāsana’ (the dispensation of the Lord Buddhā/‘Gōtama’ Buddha), in which the Noble Dhamma doctrine remained concealed.

May all heart-felt worries and troubles of the living-beings be dispelled!

May they be served with the sweet nectar of the Dhamma doctrine!

May they be relieved from the innumerable suffering of existence!

May they be appeased with the supreme peace of Nirvāna!

Sādu… sādu… sādu…!

### Foreward
The living-beings are incessantly perturbed by desire, aversion, and delusion (‘rāga, dvēśa, mōha’), and they are trapped within the pursuit of gratification (‘āsvādaya’) because of their inability to conceive the drawbacks (‘ādīnavaya’) of gratification (‘āsvādaya’).

To escape the innumerable suffering of the samsāric journey by putting an end to it, they must be able to overcome the illusion of the samsāra, by way of the Noble Dhamma doctrine apprehended by being in association with a ‘kalyāṇa mitta’ (someone who helps to wipe out the defilements off one’s mind/commonly known as a spiritual friend). This rare moment of opportunity comes forth only once for eons.

‘Gōtama’ Buddha had to undergo a multitude of suffering to provide the living-beings with this rare moment of opportunity. With the passage of time, the Noble Dhamma doctrine underwent a period of decline within the ‘Gōtama Buddha śāsana’ (the dispensation of the Lord Buddhā/‘Gōtama’ Buddha) itself. In these circumstances, the late Venerable Waharaka Abhayaratanālankāra Thero fulfilled his quest for the revival of the Noble Dhamma doctrine for the benefit of all living-beings. For this purpose, he had to undergo periods of fulfilling certain perfections to be able to revive the ‘Buddha śāsana’(the dispensation of the Lord Buddhā) this time around. Accordingly, he was able to be born to this world as a great ‘kalyāṇa mitta’ and fulfill his quest. For this reason, he can be called with the distinction of ‘Anu-Budu’ (Sub-Buddha) of these times.

The thousands of meritorious devotees who are walking the Noble Path in expectation of attaining the fruition of the Noble Path (‘mārga-phala’) are abundantly encountered with many obstacles. And sometimes they take the form of attacks from the spirit world. The late Venerable Waharaka Abhayaratanālankāra Thero too had to undergo such attacks towards the latter part of his life.

And the 52nd volume of the magazine “Heḷa Bodu Piyuma” had an article with detailed accounts of such bitter experiences faced by the late Venerable Waharaka Abhyaratanālankāra Thero, aimed at providing guidance to the readers. And that article became pivotal in shaping the purpose of preparing this booklet.

May this booklet on the worshiping of the Lord Buddhā be of great assistance to those striving to escape samsāra during this era of flourishing within the ‘Gōtama Buddha śāsana’ (the dispensation of the Lord Buddhā/‘Gōtama’ Buddha) in the backdrop of the revival of the Noble Dhamma doctrine. May it also be of great assistance to those aspiring to attain Nirvāna in a future ‘Buddha śāsana’ (the dispensation of a Lord Buddhā).

### ‘Sādukāraya’ (Acclamation/expression of admiration)
The Buddhists begin worshipping with a ‘sādukāraya’ (acclamation/expression of admiration). They did this insightfully.

“Sā” refers to the righteous conduct. With a ‘sādukāraya’ one implies that his conduct is righteous.

First ‘sādukāraya’ - **“kāyēna saṁvarō sādu”**

This signifies that killing animals, stealing, and indulging in bodily misconduct are avoided.

Second ‘sādukāraya’ - **“sādu vācāya saṁvarō”**

This signifies that lying, talebearing, harsh-speech, and frivolous-speech are avoided.

Third ‘sādukāraya’ - **“manasā saṁvarō sādu”**

This signifies that the attachment, dissent, and delusion are avoided.

Fourth ‘sādukāraya’ - **“sādu sabbatta saṁvarō”**

This signifies the acknowledgement of one’s nonassociation with ten unwholesome deeds.

“Sādu… sādu… sādu…”
